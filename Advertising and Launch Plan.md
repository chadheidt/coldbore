# Loadscope — Advertising & Launch Plan (draft 2026-05-17)

Plan now, spend after the funnel converts. This is the playbook; it is not
to be executed until commerce is live (or beta is open enough to absorb
signups). Companion to memory `project_loadscope_advertising_kickoff`.

---

## 1. Timing — when money actually goes out

| Phase | What's true | Ad spend |
|---|---|---|
| Now (pre-beta) | no funnel, request-access wall | **$0** — planning/creative only |
| Beta open | signups convertible | organic + tiny test spend |
| Commercial launch (v1.0.0, paid) | $49 funnel live | full plan, scaled to results |

Advertising into a "request beta access" wall burns money. Creative and
relationships get built now; paid spend starts at launch.

## 2. Positioning (lead with best-load)

Primary message, everywhere: **"Loadscope finds your best powder charge and
seating depth — scored from your own chronograph and target data."** Second
line: the built-in ballistic calculator / predicted DOPE, validated against
JBM, confirmed at the range. Never overclaim the solver: it is *predicted*,
you *confirm at distance*. Voice = precision-rifle vernacular, never corny.

## 3. Channel strategy (ranked — firearms-policy reality baked in)

Meta (FB/IG) and Google/YouTube **paid** ads auto-reject firearms-adjacent
creative, even software. Lean where the buyers actually are:

1. **YouTube (organic + creator sponsorships).** A 5–8 min real-workflow
   video (drag CSVs → scored ladder → suggested winner → predicted DOPE →
   pocket card). Sponsor 2–4 respected precision channels for an honest
   walkthrough. Highest-leverage channel for this niche.
2. **X / Twitter.** The precision community lives here. Organic build +
   small promoted spend (X is more tolerant than Meta/Google). Founder
   voice, real data screenshots, before/after.
3. **Endemic.** Sniper's Hide, AccurateShooter, r/precisionrifle &
   r/handloading, PRS/NRL channels, podcasts (be a useful contributor for
   weeks *before* mentioning the app — this community punishes spam).
4. **Reviewer/influencer seeding.** Free licenses to ~5 trusted reviewers;
   one or two covering it moves more than any ad.
5. **Email list** from beta + site — owned channel, launch sequence.
6. **Paid Meta/Google:** assume hostile; only test cautiously with
   software-only creative; do not depend on it.

## 4. Sample ads (precision-rifle voice — adjust at launch)

> Loadscope — it scores every charge and seating depth on velocity
> flat-spot, SD, group and vertical, and tells you which load actually won.
> Then it predicts your DOPE to 1000. Mac (Windows soon). loadscope.app

**YouTube sponsor read (host script):**

> drop in your Xero and target exports, it scores every load objectively,

**Endemic/forum (only after being a real contributor):**
> Built this because I was tired of hand-typing velocities and second-guessing

> COPY RULE for all ad/marketing text: precision-rifle vernacular, never
> corny; lead with best-load. Banned words — never use: *node*, *"before you

**YouTube demo video outline (5–8 min):** cold open on the pain (messy
spreadsheet) → drag CSVs, watch it fill → the scored ladder + suggested
winner → seating-depth pass → predicted DOPE table → print the pocket card
→ "your data, your range card" close + URL.

## 5. Budget framework (lean, post-launch, results-gated)

- **Month 1–2 (launch):** ~$0–500 — YouTube video production + 1 creator
  sponsorship test + reviewer seed copies. Mostly time, not cash.
- **Scale only on proven CAC:** at $49 (net ~$46), a sale must cost well
  under ~$20 acquired to be worth scaling. Track per-channel. Pour into
  whatever channel returns under that; kill the rest.
- No big upfront ad budget. This niche rewards trust and word-of-mouth, not
  spend.

## 6. Revenue & market-size model

**Market:** niche-within-niche — handloads **and** owns a chronograph
**and** does systematic load development **and** (today) Mac. Realistically
tens of thousands of serious prospects, not millions. High-intent,
high-trust, community-driven. **Windows ≈ 5x** the addressable base (most
reloaders are Windows) — the single biggest lever.

**Unit economics:** $49 one-time, net **~$46** after Lemon Squeezy (~5% +
~$0.50; verify current rate). One-time, not recurring → revenue = units, no
MRR.

**Scenario table (lifetime, rough, assumption-heavy — NOT a promise):**

| Units | Net (~$46) | Read |
|---|---|---|
| 100 | ~$4,600 | soft launch / Mac-only, weak distribution |
| 500 | ~$23,000 | decent community traction, Mac-only |
| 1,000 | ~$46,000 | good word-of-mouth + Windows |
| 5,000 | ~$230,000 | strong distribution + Windows + endemic reach |

**Honest read:** realistically a meaningful supplemental-income indie
product (low-to-mid five figures over its life is plausible; six figures
needs Windows + unusually strong distribution or a recurring tier).
Outcome is **distribution-bound, not product-bound** — the product is
already a strength. Levers, in order: Windows → community/endemic reach →
recurring/data tier later → broader "long-range shooter" positioning.

## 7. Strategic partnerships & potential acquirers

- **Garmin** — *not a deal.* Too large; builds in-house; a pre/low-revenue
  Mac utility is a rounding error. The realistic prize is **ecosystem
  credibility**: become the recognized third-party load-dev companion the
  Xero community points newcomers to. Earned via users/community, not a
  handshake.
- **BallisticX** — *the realistic partner/combine candidate.* Small,
  complementary, exact same shooter; you consume their target output.
  Possible: cross-promotion, bundle, data-format collaboration, even a
  small merger. A BallisticX API/account hook would also enable true
  zero-file sync (a real product win).
- **Other endemic players** — chronograph/target-app makers, PRS/optics
  brands, ballistics-software companies: a *strategic* acquirer beats a
  financial flipper.
- **Leverage timing — the rule:** none of this happens pre-traction.
  Companies join up with things that already have users, revenue, and
  community standing. Build the base first; negotiate from strength
  post-launch. Keep pluggable-parser breadth (LabRadar/OnTarget/ShotMarker/
  SMT) as both a vendor-lock-in hedge and a negotiating chip.

## 8. Launch sequence

1. Pre-beta Step 4 done (descriptions/social aligned to new pitch).
2. Beta open; gather testimonials + the email list; seed reviewers.
3. Commerce flips on (v1.0.0, EULA lawyer-reviewed, business/entity gate).
4. Windows shipped (or imminent — biggest reach unlock).
5. YouTube video live + 1–2 creator sponsorships + endemic posts + email
   launch sequence, same week.
6. Measure CAC per channel weekly; double down on sub-$20-CAC channels,
   cut the rest. Iterate copy from what converts.

## 9. Bottom line

Strong, differentiated product in a small high-value niche. Money is gated
by distribution + Windows + community trust, not by the app. Plan is ready;
spend starts at launch; partnerships are a post-traction conversation.

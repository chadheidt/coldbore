Loadscope vs. Ballistic-X: Which One Actually Fits Your Load Development?

Ballistic-X is a cheap, well-liked phone app for measuring your target groups — a lot of shooters already have it. It's a genuinely different tool than Loadscope, though: it measures groups, and that's it. Your chronograph data still lives somewhere else, and so does the job of deciding which charge actually won. Here's the honest comparison.

These two aren't really head-to-head competitors — they solve different halves of the same problem. Ballistic-X measures what's on the target. Loadscope measures the target and reads your chronograph, then scores the two together and calls your best load. Which one you need depends on how much of that job you want done for you.

What Ballistic-X does well

Ballistic-X is a solid, well-established phone app (iOS and Android) for measuring shot groups — photograph your target, mark your reference size and your shots, and it gives you group size, and it'll help you zero your rifle with its ATZ (Adjustment To Zero) feature. It's a one-time $7.99 for the core app, no subscription required for the essentials, and it's right there on your phone at the bench — no extra gear to carry.

Where it's limited

  • No chronograph data at all. Ballistic-X measures what's on paper. Your velocity, SD, and ES from your chronograph live somewhere else — a spreadsheet, a notebook, or your memory.
  • It doesn't call a winner. It measures each group, but weighing that against velocity consistency across a whole ladder to pick your best charge or seating depth is still on you.
  • Advanced stats and export are a subscription. The core app is a one-time buy, but the Advanced Toolkit (more detailed analysis, data export) is a separate $9.99/year.
  • Phone-only. There's no desktop workbook to open later, sort, or build on.

What Loadscope adds

  • Reads your chronograph directly — Garmin Xero, LabRadar, and more — alongside your measured groups, not as a separate step.
  • Measures your groups from a photo too, right in the same app — group size, mean radius, point of impact.
  • Scores and names your best load — weighing the velocity flat-spot, SD, mean radius, and vertical across every charge and seating depth, instead of leaving the call to you.
  • Predicts your DOPE and prints a pocket range card for the load it picks.
  • One-time $59, no subscription, ever — not for the basics, not for the advanced stats.
  • A real Mac/Windows desktop workbook you own, plus a free phone companion for measuring at the bench and carrying your DOPE to the line.

So which is right for you?

If all you need is a quick, cheap way to measure a group at the bench and you're happy tracking velocity and picking your load some other way, Ballistic-X does that job well for not much money. If you want your chronograph and your target groups scored together — and you'd rather the tool name your best load and print your DOPE than do that math yourself — that's the gap Loadscope was built to close.

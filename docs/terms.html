3. Reloading & shooting safety

Loadscope is a tool for organizing and analyzing data you collect during precision rifle load development. It is NOT a source of published load data — it does not supply start or maximum charges, cartridge overall lengths, or load recipes; obtain those only from current reloading manuals. When Loadscope highlights a "suggested" winning charge or seating depth, that is a statistical ranking of the loads you personally tested — not a safety-vetted recommendation of what is safe to load or fire. Predicted ballistic data is an estimate, not a measurement; confirm it by live fire at known distances before relying on it for any shot. Always cross-reference loads against published manuals from powder, bullet, and cartridge manufacturers, watch for pressure signs, and start below maximum and work up.

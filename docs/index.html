How Loadscope works.

Loadscope works from the data you collect at the range. Four things decide your best load: velocity flat-spot and shot-to-shot SD from your chronograph; mean radius (or group size), plus vertical SD, from your target app. Here's the whole process:

1

Drop your CSVs

Drag your CSV exports onto the window and everything fills itself in — no retyping. (Currently supported: Garmin Xero and BallisticX.) Using other gear? Send a sample CSV and we'll add it.

2

Your best powder charge and seating depth

Loadscope scores every load you tested across those metrics and determines your best powder charge and seating depth — each tagged at the top of its log.

3

Built-in ballistic calculator

From your winning load and the muzzle velocity measured by your chronograph, Loadscope's solver predicts elevation, wind, drop, velocity and energy at every distance — validated against the industry-standard JBM.

4

Predicted DOPE & Pocket Card

It prints on a 4×6 Pocket Range Card: predicted to start, then you confirm each distance by live fire and your real numbers replace the predictions.

How do I get an access code?

Loadscope is in private beta. Email support@loadscope.app to request access. You'll receive a unique code that unlocks both the download and the app itself.

How accurate is the predicted DOPE?

Loadscope uses a G7/G1 point-mass ballistic solver — the same model class as Strelok, GeoBallistics and the free Hornady/Berger calculators — validated against the industry-standard JBM solver (within ~0.07 mil with good inputs). Measured-drag solvers like Applied Ballistics and Hornady 4DOF pull ahead deep in the transonic. Predicted values are an estimate, not a measurement — always confirm by live fire at known distances before relying on them for a shot that matters.

What's "predicted" vs "confirmed"?

Grey italic numbers are predicted by the solver from your bullet, muzzle velocity and conditions. At the range you type what you actually dialed — that row turns solid white = confirmed. Predictions never overwrite confirmed data, and your printed Pocket Card carries a "verify at the range" banner until a row is confirmed.

Do I have to enter a ballistic coefficient?

34 of the most popular match bullets have BCs built in. For any other bullet, type its BC off the box or the maker's site once. It's not a source of published load data — you still cross-reference charges against current reloading manuals. The bullet database grows over time with no app update needed.

Does it work with [other chronograph]?

Currently supports Garmin Xero (ShotView CSVs) and BallisticX. The architecture is pluggable — adding LabRadar, MagnetoSpeed, Athlon Rangecraft, etc. is straightforward. If you'd like one added, send a sample CSV to support@loadscope.app.

Is my data going to the cloud?

No. Loadscope writes to a regular .xlsx file on your Mac. No account, no sync, no telemetry. Your loads stay on your machine.

Is Loadscope code-signed by Apple?

Yes. Loadscope is signed with a Developer ID Application certificate and notarized by Apple. macOS will open it without security warnings on the first launch — the same as any commercial Mac app.

Does it work on Apple Silicon?

Yes — runs natively on Intel Macs and via Rosetta 2 on M-series. Performance is fine; Loadscope is a small app.

What about Windows / iOS?

Both ports are planned. Mac is the current focus; Windows is next on the roadmap. iOS is a longer-term project.

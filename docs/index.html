Install in three steps.

Tested on Sonoma and Sequoia. About one minute from download to first import.

1

Download

Click the Download button at the top of the page. Save the zip file (about 57 MB).

2

Drag to Applications

Unzip. Drag Cold Bore.app into your Applications folder. The Quick Start guide is in the same zip.

3

Right-click → Open

The first time you launch, right-click the app and choose Open. macOS will ask once; click Open again. Future launches are normal.

Beta note: Cold Bore is in beta. The right-click → Open step is a macOS quirk that goes away once we're code-signed (rolling out shortly). Until then, this one-time step is normal for indie Mac apps.

Common questions.

If you don't see your question here, email coldboreapp@gmail.com.

What's the workflow look like in practice?

Range trip → CSV from your Xero, CSV from BallisticX → drop both onto Cold Bore → workbook opens in Excel with all the velocity, SD, ES, group size, mean radius numbers filled in. You add notes, save, done.

Does it work with [other chronograph]?

Currently supports Garmin Xero (ShotView CSVs) and BallisticX. The architecture is pluggable — adding LabRadar, MagnetoSpeed, Athlon Rangecraft, etc. is straightforward. If you'd like one added, send a sample CSV to coldboreapp@gmail.com.

Is my data going to the cloud?

No. Cold Bore writes to a regular .xlsx file on your Mac. No account, no sync, no telemetry. Your loads stay on your machine.

Why does my Mac say "Apple cannot check this for malicious software"?

Standard message for indie apps without an Apple Developer signature ($99/yr). The right-click → Open trick is the documented Apple-approved bypass. Code-signing is on the immediate roadmap.

Does it work on Apple Silicon?

Yes — runs natively on Intel Macs and via Rosetta 2 on M-series. Performance is fine; Cold Bore is a small app.

What about Windows / iOS?

Both ports are planned. Mac is the current focus; Windows is next on the roadmap. iOS is a longer-term project.

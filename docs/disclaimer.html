What Loadscope is — and isn't

Loadscope is a tool for organizing and analyzing data you collect during precision rifle load development. It is NOT a source of published load data — it does not supply start or maximum charges, cartridge overall lengths, or load recipes. Obtain those only from current reloading manuals published by the powder, bullet, and cartridge manufacturers.

About the "suggested" winner

When Loadscope highlights a "suggested" winning charge or seating depth, that is a statistical ranking of the loads you personally tested — not a safety-vetted recommendation of what is safe to load or fire. It tells you which of your own tested loads scored best on the metrics you measured; it does not tell you a load is safe.

Predicted ballistic data

Predicted ballistic data (elevation, wind, drop, velocity, energy) is an estimate, not a measurement. Confirm it by live fire at known distances before relying on it for any shot.

Safe handloading practices

  • Always cross-reference loads against published manuals from powder, bullet, and cartridge manufacturers.
  • Watch for pressure signs.
  • Start below maximum and work up.
